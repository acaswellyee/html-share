Methodology

Analysis Approach

  1. Data Collection: Extracted 45,957 unique part descriptions from Precinmac's ERP system (Vendor_PN.csv)
  2. Pattern Recognition: Analyzed part description text to identify item categories (collets, cutting tools, raw materials, services, etc.)
  3. Quantitative Analysis: Counted occurrences of each category type to determine inventory composition
  4. Gap Identification: Cross-referenced inventory categories against both taxonomies to find:
    • Gaps: Item types with no clear taxonomy home
    • Overlaps: Items that could map to 2+ categories (AI confusion risk)
  5. Structural Analysis: Evaluated hierarchy consistency, depth, and machine-parseability
  6. Recommendations: Generated specific additions/modifications for each taxonomy with detailed subcategory structure

⚠️ Questions for Precinmac Team

Critical Classification Questions

1. 3D Printing Materials Usage (3,600 parts, 7.8% of inventory)

Observation: Large volume of PLA, ABS, CPE filaments, print cores, and build plates detected.

Question: Precinmac is a precision CNC/Swiss machining operation. Are these 3D printing materials:

  • Used for prototyping/tooling fixtures?
  • For a separate business unit/division?
  • Legacy inventory no longer used?
  • Incorrectly categorized items?

Impact: Affects whether to include "3D PRINTING SUPPLIES" category in final taxonomy. Both improved taxonomies currently include this category pending clarification.

2. Production Components vs. MRO Classification

Question: How does Precinmac distinguish between:

  • Production Components: Fasteners, bearings, O-rings, electronics that become part of customer deliverables
  • MRO Supplies: The same items used for machine maintenance and facility operations

Background: PCM improved taxonomy has detailed COMPONENTS and SUBASSEMBLIES categories. SpendAI taxonomy treats most of these as MRO items only.

Impact: Determines whether SpendAI taxonomy needs a new top-level COMPONENTS category or if all such items should remain under MRO/OEM.

⚠️ Overlap & Multi-Classification Risks

Items that could map to multiple categories, causing AI confusion:

  • Fasteners: 3-Way Ambiguity

    Current: Components > Fasteners: "Screws, Bolts, Rivets, Washers, Nuts, Retaining Rings"

    Overlap Risk: Could also map to MRO > Shop Supplies OR MRO > Spare Parts

    Example Confusion: "Hex Bolt M6x20" — is it a production component or maintenance supply?

    Fix: Split into "Production Fasteners" (Components) and "Maintenance Fasteners" (MRO)

  • Tubing/Hose: 3-Way Ambiguity

    Current: Components > Tubing & Hoses: "PVC Tubing, Steel Braided Hose, Air Hose"

    Overlap Risk: Could be Raw Materials > Polymers & Plastics OR MRO > Shop Supplies

    Example Confusion: "3/4 IDx 1 OD CLEAR TUBING" — engineered assembly or bulk stock?

    Fix: Components = engineered assemblies with fittings, Raw Materials = bulk tubing stock, MRO = replacement hoses

  • Adhesives: 3-Way Ambiguity

    Current: Raw Materials > Chemicals: "Acids, Solvents, Adhesives, Coolants, Paint Thinners"

    Overlap Risk: Could be MRO > Shop Supplies OR Components (if part of assembly)

    Example Confusion: "LOCTITE 242 THREAD LOCKER" — raw material chemical or shop supply?

    Fix: Bulk adhesives (gallons) = Raw Materials, Small containers = MRO Shop Supplies

  • O-Rings & Seals: 3-Way Ambiguity

    Current: Components > Seals > O-ring

    Overlap Risk: Could be MRO > Spare Parts OR Raw Materials > Rubber & Elastomers

    Example Confusion: "O-RING,2-206,VITON" — engineered seal, replacement part, or rubber material?

    Fix: Engineered O-ring assemblies = Components, Bulk O-ring cord = Raw Materials, Replacement O-rings = MRO

  • Electrical vs Electronic Components

    Current: Separate categories exist but boundary unclear

    Overlap Risk: Relays, Switches could be either electrical or electronic

    Fix: Merge into "Electronics & Electrical" OR create clear voltage/application boundary

Clinical & Medical · L2–L4 Taxonomy Design

Clinical & Medical
Proposed Structure

Domain-first taxonomy for patient-care-facing spend. Organized by product type at L2, clinical specialty or use-case at L3, and specific item at L4. Designed for reliable AI classification from invoice descriptions alone.

L2 Logic
Product type — what kind of thing it is. The AI anchors here first. Consumable vs. implant vs. equipment vs. service are fundamentally different supplier markets and contract structures.
L3 Logic
Clinical specialty or use-case — where it's used in the hospital. This is where the Stryker test applies: orthopedic implants and cardiac implants are different L3s even though both are implants.
L4 Logic
Specific item type — durable enough that a buyer recognizes it, not so granular it fragments on wording differences. No brand names. No product model numbers.

L2 · 01
Medical Consumables
Single-use or limited-use clinical supplies consumed at the point of care. These are not permanently implanted and are typically ordered through a GPO distributor (Medline, Cardinal, Owens & Minor) on high-frequency purchase orders.
Why separate from Implants? Consumables are replenished constantly, managed through par-level inventory, and contracted via GPO distribution agreements. Implants are case-specific, physician-preference driven, and negotiated directly with manufacturers. Collapsing them obscures both cost and contract strategy.

Kept in Medical Consumables not Facilities.
Boundary Rules for Medical Consumables
  • Clinical PPE (gloves, gowns, masks) → Medical Consumables · Nursing & General Care. Facility/operations PPE (hard hats, hi-vis) → Facilities & Operations.
  • Hemostatic agents → Medical Consumables · Surgical & OR Supplies if single-use topical. If surgically implanted (pledgets, permanent hemostatic devices) → Implants & Devices.
  • Feeding pumps → Medical Consumables · Clinical Nutrition (the pump is typically a low-cost disposable or rental). High-value infusion pumps → Medical Equipment · Patient Care Equipment.
L2 · 02
Implants & Devices
Products that are permanently or semi-permanently placed inside a patient's body, or high-value single-use devices that are integral to a surgical procedure. Typically physician-preference items negotiated directly with manufacturers under system or GPO contracts.
Why separate from Medical Consumables? Implants are case-specific, physician-driven, and the #1 area for cost variation in surgical spend. They require different contracting strategies (physician preference item programs, value analysis committee review) and different AI classification signals — the item description will typically include words like "implant," "prosthesis," "graft," "stent," or a specific anatomical reference.

Highest spend concentration of any implant category at most health systems. Separate L3 ensures Wellstar can track cost-per-case for joint replacement programs specifically.

Grouping by product type rather than specialty avoids fragmentation.
Neurology & Spine Devices Spinal Cord Stimulation Devices Neurovascular IR Products Deep Brain Stimulation Devices Intracranial Pressure Monitors Vertebral Compression Fracture Products From MINOR CAPITAL · SURGERY neuro items Distinct enough from orthopedic spine (which is fixation hardware) to warrant its own L3. Neurovascular IR is a high-growth, high-cost area for health systems expanding stroke programs.
Specialty Implants Urological Implants Ophthalmic Implants ENT Implants Auditory Implants Maxillofacial Implants Implantable Infusion Ports Contraceptive Implants From MINOR CAPITAL · SURGERY specialty items Lower volume implant categories that don't warrant individual L3 nodes at most health systems. Grouped here to avoid an "Other" catch-all while keeping the tree manageable.
Boundary Rules for Implants & Devices
  • The implantability test: If the item is permanently or semi-permanently placed inside the patient's body → Implants & Devices. If it is used during a procedure and discarded → Medical Consumables.
  • Vascular grafts → Cardiovascular Implants (implanted permanently). Vascular closure devices (used post-procedure, not implanted) → Medical Consumables · Surgical & OR Supplies.
  • Hemostatic agents → If absorbable and left in the body → Soft Tissue & Biologic Implants. If topical and removed → Medical Consumables.
  • Ventricular assist devices → Cardiovascular Implants (implanted). Temporary external VADs → Medical Equipment · Patient Care Equipment.
L2 · 03
Medical Equipment
Durable medical equipment and systems used for diagnosis, treatment, or monitoring. Includes both large installed systems (imaging, surgical robotics) and smaller capital devices at the bedside or point of care. Equipment is purchased, leased, or rented — not consumed.
Why this replaces Major Capital in this domain? The equipment is identifiable from the invoice description. Whether it is capitalized depends on cost and accounting policy — not something the AI can determine. By putting equipment in its own L2 with descriptive L3/L4 nodes, finance can filter these rows manually for capital reporting until tagging is available.

Keeping cardiology equipment distinct from general surgical equipment reflects how Wellstar's service line structure actually works.

Placing it here (rather than in Pharmacy L1) because it is durable equipment — the purchase pattern and vendor market mirrors medical equipment more than drug procurement.
Boundary Rules for Medical Equipment
  • Equipment vs. consumable: If it is reused across multiple patients or procedures → Medical Equipment. If it is single-use or limited-use → Medical Consumables.
  • Contrast media injectors → Medical Equipment · Imaging Equipment (the injector is durable). Contrast media itself (the fluid) → Medical Consumables · Imaging & Radiology Supplies.
  • Rental or lease of equipment → still classifies to the same L3 as a purchase. The rental vs. purchase distinction is a spend-type attribute, not a classification decision.
L2 · 04
Laboratory
All laboratory-specific supplies, reagents, analyzers, and outsourced testing services. Lab is large enough and distinct enough at a health system to warrant its own L2 — it has a completely separate vendor market, GPO contract structure, and often a separate procurement team.
Why separate from Medical Consumables and Equipment? Lab spend blends consumables (reagents, collection tubes), equipment (analyzers), and services (reference lab) in ways that don't neatly map to the other L2 product-type categories. Lab vendors like Roche, Becton Dickinson, and Quest span all three types — keeping Lab as its own L2 means these vendors always land in the same place regardless of what they're invoicing for.

Kept in Lab L2 rather than Clinical Services because the buyer, the contract, and the GPO relationship are all lab-specific. The service vs. supply distinction is less important than keeping lab spend together.
L2 · 05
Clinical Services
Outsourced clinical care services delivered by third-party providers — where Wellstar is purchasing a clinical outcome or capability rather than a product. Distinct from staffing (which is people) and from lab services (which stay in the Lab L2).
The key distinction: Clinical Services are vendor-delivered care episodes or programs. The vendor brings the clinical expertise, protocol, and often the equipment. Staffing (travel nurses, locum physicians) is different — Wellstar is buying labor that works under its own supervision. That distinction belongs in People & Workforce.

Biomedical & Clinical Engineering Services Biomedical Equipment Maintenance Clinical Equipment Repair Surgical Instrument Reprocessing Medical Device Calibration Services From MEDICAL SERVICES · BIOMEDICAL + SURGERY repair Biomedical engineering services are clinical in nature — they maintain the equipment that delivers care. Kept here rather than in Facilities because the vendor market (Sodexo HTM, Aramark Healthcare Technologies, Crothall) is healthcare-specific and often managed by clinical engineering departments.
Boundary Rules for Clinical Services
  • Outsourced anesthesia group → Clinical Services · Specialty & Acute Care. A CRNA placed through a staffing agency working under Wellstar supervision → People & Workforce · Physician & Advanced Practice Staffing.
  • Contract physical therapists → if the vendor runs the therapy program → Clinical Services · Diagnostic & Therapeutic. If they are individual contractors working under Wellstar → People & Workforce.
  • Patient nutrition services → Clinical nutrition products (enteral formula) → Medical Consumables. Registered dietitian services outsourced to a vendor → Clinical Services · Diagnostic & Therapeutic.
L2 · 06
Sterile Processing & Infection Control
Supplies, equipment, and services related to sterilization, disinfection, and infection prevention across clinical settings. A distinct procurement category with its own vendor market and compliance requirements (Joint Commission, CDC, AAMI standards).
Why a separate L2? Sterilization and infection control spend cuts across nursing, surgery, and facilities but belongs to none of them cleanly. It has its own vendor base (Steris, Getinge, 3M), its own regulatory standards, and is typically managed by the sterile processing department. Giving it its own L2 prevents it from being split between Medical Consumables and Facilities.
L3 L4 Items Design Rationale
Sterilization Products & Equipment Steam Sterilizers (Autoclaves) Low Temperature Sterilization Systems Washer-Decontaminators Sterilization Wrap & Pouches Instrument Containers & Trays Sterilization Assurance & Indicators From MAJOR CAPITAL + SUPPLIES · SURGERY sterilization items Steris and Getinge sell both the capital sterilizers and the consumable wrap/pouches. Keeping both in the same L3 means vendor spend aggregates correctly regardless of what line item they're invoicing.
Infection Prevention & Disinfection High-Level Disinfectants Surface Disinfection Wipes & Solutions Instrument Cleaners & Enzymatics Hand Hygiene Products (Clinical) CHG Skin Prep Products UV Disinfection Systems Air & Environmental Infection Control From NURSING disinfection + environmental control items Clinical disinfection products (high-level disinfectants, CHG preps) stay here. Facility janitorial cleaning products (floor cleaner, general surface wipes) go to Facilities & Operations. The distinction is whether the product meets clinical antimicrobial standards or is a general-purpose cleaner.
